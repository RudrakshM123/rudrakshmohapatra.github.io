Overview of my work

My research experience is in the field of environmental sciences. I mainly focus on using geospatial tools such as Geographic Information System and Remote Sensing data to solve problems pertaining to environmental systems. Some of my works:

  • 2020: I worked on measuring the water potential of a semi-arid region in central India, trying to throw light onto the different sources of water, how they can be preserved, as rapid urbanization engulfed the rural resources.
    It has been published in the Social Science Research Network SSRN
  • 2021: I created a machine learning integrated geospatial software to compute landslide susceptibility of an area
    The project was awarded 2nd Best Poster Presentation award for original research at iGeomatics, a national symposioum on geo-informatics hosted by the Indian Society of Remoste Sensing and Indian Society of Geomatics.
    It also received the RICOH Sustainable Development award at the IRIS National Fair, among 100 STEM projects selected nationally by the Govt. of India.
  • 2022: I published a research paper on calculating the soil erosion of a vulnerable river basin in central India. Using satellite data and geospatial software, while executing a soil erosion model, I calculated the annual rate at which the topsoil was being eroded.
    I had presented this work previously in a conference hosted by the International Geographical Union held in China.
    It has been published in Springer Modelling and Earth Systems Sciences
  • 2022: I will be presenting my work on hazard detection using Ensemble Tree Based Algorithms and Geospatial Application at the American Geophysical Union this December.
